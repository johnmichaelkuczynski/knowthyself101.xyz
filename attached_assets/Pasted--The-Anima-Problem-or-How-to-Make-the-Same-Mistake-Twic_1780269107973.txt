# The Anima Problem, or How to Make the Same Mistake Twice and Worse

The previous note showed that the "shadow" explains nothing, because it personifies a network of specific drives and defenses into a single humor, and personification destroys the fine grain on which all psychological explanation depends. The anima and the animus commit the identical crime—and then commit a second one on top of it, which is why they are even more useless.

Start with the man. Smith is a macho man, and not merely in being heterosexual: he values toughness, self-reliance, and logic, and he really does value them. This is largely an authentic expression of who he is. But "largely" is not "wholly." Some of the toughness is also a defense—a bad childhood, no one to fall back on, softness that was not survivable. And, a separate matter, the value system sometimes makes him override what he actually wants in the moment: he would like to jerk off and do coke, and the severe masculine code tells him to suck it up and go for a jog. So the true picture is a single man carrying several drives and several values, sometimes doing what he wants, sometimes not, sometimes defending against a real threat and sometimes against a phantom, sometimes overcompensating to good effect and sometimes to bad. Every term in that description is doing work. The explanation lives entirely in the fine grain.

Now watch Jung obliterate it, in two strokes.

The first stroke is the broad-strokes crime, the Marx crime. Jung takes "masculine" and "feminine" as monolithic blocks and treats them as explanatory primitives. But "Smith is masculine" explains nothing about Smith, because the work is never done at that altitude. What explains Smith is the *bundle*—the authentic temperamental bent, plus the defensive overlay, plus the arbitrating code—three things with three origins and three dynamics. "Masculinity" is a label smeared across all of them, and a label smeared that wide cannot pick out the part that is actually lifting. It is "class interest" spread across the yuppie and the union boss: a word that fits everything and therefore distinguishes nothing. Granting, as one should, that masculine and feminine natures are perfectly real, they are still useless as primitives, because reality at this level is built out of specifics and the block-categories paint straight over them.

The second stroke is the personification crime, and here the anima outdoes even the shadow. Take the true fine-grained picture and now turn the pieces into little people: call the toughness his "real self," call the suppressed softness his "anima," call the disowned appetite his "shadow." What, exactly, has been asserted? There are only two readings, and both are idiotic. Either (a) the macho is a fraud and Smith *really* wants to be a ballerina, not a lumberjack—which is simply false, since the toughness is largely authentic; or (b) there is a literal second occupant in there, a little man or little woman who wants the ballerina life on his or her own account—which is to diagnose multiple personality disorder by grammar alone. The force model never faces this fork. Smith wants toughness *and* wants to get high; the wants conflict; the code arbitrates, sometimes well and sometimes badly; one man, plural drives, no second tenant. But the instant you convert a drive into a self, that self must want something in its own name, and then it is either lying about Smith or it is a separate patient. You have called the man a fraud or called him a crowd, and he is neither.

This is the whole disease in one organism. The shadow personified one disowned trait and lost the explanation. The anima personifies a disowned trait *and* dresses it in a block-category that was never explanatory to begin with—coarse anthropology wrapped around a homunculus. It fails at both levels at once. And the lesson generalizes past Jung: any psychology that answers "what is this man doing and why" by introducing additional little people inside him has not gone deeper, it has quit. The depth was always in the specifics. Personify them and the depth is precisely what you throw away.
